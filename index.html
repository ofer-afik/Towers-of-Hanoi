Towers of Hanoi

Overview

Basically, there are three pegs, and a number of disks with varying disk sizes. The disks all start on one peg, from big to small, and you need to move all the disks to another peg, usually from the left-most to the right-most. Of course, it's a bit more complicated. You can read the rules down below.

Rules

These are the rules we will play with:

  • To restart, just press start game again

  • To move a disk in this website, press on the tower name/button you want to move the disk from, then on the tower name/button you want to move to

  • To win, you must move all the disks from tower I to tower III

  • Each move consists of taking the topmost disk from a peg and placing it on another

  • You cannot place a disk in such a way that there is a smaller disk below a bigger disk

  • Your number of moves and time will be counted, you can view it.
Mathematical Expression

To figure out the minimum number of moves required to complete the puzzle, we must calculate 2n - 1. That means that a game with 3 disks will take a minimum of 7 moves, and a game with 8 will take a minimum of 255 moves.

The Legend

There is a legend that says there is a temple in which there are priests tasked with completing this puzzle, with 3 poles and 64 disks! According to our expression, that will take 18,446,744,073,709,551,616 moves. Assuming that each move takes one second, it will take 584.5 billion years!
